## Investment Planning Tool Framework

### Purpose
Create clarity on investment strategy by defining your "buy box" based on expected returns and investment types (cashflow vs. growth).

### Two Phases of Investment Strategy

**Phase 1: Growth Phase**
- Dedicated to growing Y (building wealth)
- Focus on capital appreciation
- Higher risk tolerance acceptable

**Phase 2: Maintenance Phase**
- Stabilization using Y (living off it)
- Focus on income generation
- Lower risk tolerance needed

**Note:** These phases can cycle - you can build passive income allowing for risk-free growth

### Key Variables

- **X** = Monthly living amount needed in the future
- **Y** = Total capital needed to produce X per month
- **Z** = Expected rate of return for Y to produce X monthly
- **A** = Time until you need that monthly return
- **G** = Rate of return needed to reach Y
- **B** = Current retirement balance
- **C** = Retirement growth rate

### Investment Decision Factors

**Current Situation Assessment:**
1. Current retirement balance and growth rate
2. Available cash for investment
3. Current investments and performance
4. Current income (to calculate additional supplementation)
5. Current savings amount (calculating future capital available)

**The 4 Dials Calculator:**
1. **Time** - How long until you need the returns?
2. **Amount** - How much do you need to generate?
3. **Risk/Return** - What rate of return is needed? (Tied together)
4. **Tax Concern** - Current and future tax implications

### Potential Investment Vehicles

**Real Estate:**
- Multi-family (Class A, Value Add, New Construction)
- Single Family Rentals (Turn-key)
- Short Term Rentals

**Other Vehicles:**
- Hedge Funds
- Fixed Funds
- Private Equity

### Decision Framework

1. **Determine X** - Monthly living expenses needed
2. **Calculate Y** - Capital needed to produce X at Z return rate
3. **Assess Time (A)** - When do you need this income?
4. **Calculate G** - Growth rate needed to reach Y in time A
5. **Consider withdrawals** - Will you pull money out during growth phase?
6. **Tax strategy** - Choose vehicles based on tax efficiency
7. **Risk assessment** - Match risk tolerance to required returns

**Output:** Investment strategy report with specific vehicle recommendations and action steps
