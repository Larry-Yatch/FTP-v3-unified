### **Design Philosophy: Why We Removed These**

**Principle 1: No Duplication**
- If Tool 1 collected it (mindset, trauma, money beliefs), we don't ask again

- Focus on awareness levels, not granular data
- "Do you track?" is more important than "What are the exact amounts?"
- GPT can generate insights without perfect precision

**Principle 3: Actionability**
- Past financial history (10 years ago) doesn't inform current actions
- Future speculation (5-10 years) is too abstract
- Focus on present clarity and immediate next steps

**Principle 4: Cognitive Load**
- 64 questions ‚Üí Too exhausting, leads to abandonment
- 57 questions ‚Üí Manageable, maintains engagement
- Psychological questions at end allow depth after building trust

**Principle 5: GPT-Enhanced Personalization**
- With GPT analysis, 8 free-text responses generate rich insights
- Without GPT, those questions would be wasted
- Balance quantitative structure with qualitative depth

## üé® Question Design Notes

### **Scale Consistency: -5 to +5 (No Zero)**

**Why No Zero:**
- Forces intentionality (no neutral fence-sitting)
- Cleaner data interpretation (negative = struggle, positive = strength)
- Better segmentation for insights (below 0 = needs work, above 0 = functioning)

**Scale Anchoring:**
- **-5:** Extreme negative (crisis, avoidance, dysfunction)
- **-3:** Significant challenge (struggling, inconsistent)
- **-1:** Mild challenge (aware of issues, trying)
- **+1:** Mild strength (functioning, room for improvement)
- **+3:** Significant strength (systematic, confident)
- **+5:** Mastery (optimized, strategic, zero stress)

### **Stress Questions Strategy**

**Added stress questions for ALL 5 domains:**
- Income (Q17)
- Spending (Q22)
- Debt (Q28)
- Emergency Fund (Q34)
- Savings (Q38)
- Investments (Q42)
- Retirement (Q47)
- Insurance (Q51)

**Why critical:**
- Stress weighting is core to priority algorithm
- Emotional impact matters more than raw scores
- Spending stress (weight=5) vs retirement stress (weight=1) affects prioritization
- Students with high stress + low clarity need different interventions than low stress + low clarity
